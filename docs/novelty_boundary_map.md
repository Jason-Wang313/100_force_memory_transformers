# Novelty Boundary Map

## Crowded Territory

- Generic robot transformers.
- More imitation data.
- Standard recurrent memory.
- Generic uncertainty-aware MPC.
- Conformal safety filters.
- Online residual system identification.
- Tactile or force feedback without a persistent hidden-state object.
- Contact-mode HMMs and particle-filter force-belief controllers.
- Adaptive impedance and robust force-threshold controllers.

## Claimed Boundary

The local contribution is a persistent, calibrated force-memory state for hidden contact regimes. It is not just a larger transformer or a safety wrapper; it combines explicit force-event memory, decay, reset gates, diagnostic probes, calibration, and tail-risk planning.

## Current Evidence

The boundary is locally supported because v5 beats system-ID, impedance, particle-filter, conformal, recurrent, force-history, and robust-threshold references under hidden-force shifts, and because removed-component ablations reduce success, utility, or safety. The 2026-06-22 v5 rerun reproduced this pattern under a larger protocol.

## Boundary Still Not Proven Externally

The claim is not yet proven against trained tactile transformers, real robot system-ID pipelines, accepted high-fidelity manipulation benchmarks, or real manipulation data.
